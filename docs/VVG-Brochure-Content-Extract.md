## → About page

### Vision
Bhārata, also known as India, has a proud history of being the birthplace of some of the most ancient and rich knowledge systems in the world. The country has been blessed with the sacred Vedic Adhyayanayoga, which is a highly revered spiritual knowledge of this holy land; it is our sacred duty and responsibility to ensure that this vast treasure of Vedic knowledge is preserved and passed on to our future generations so that they too can benefit from this timeless wisdom and lead fulfilling lives.

Janaseva Trust is known for its relentless service in the field of education for over 52 years. With this background, Veda Vijnana Gurukulam was established in the month of Jyeṣṭha, Īśvarasaṃvatsara (i.e. Veda Vijnana Gurukulam's *rsi-jñāna*-based education for the progress of the Nation, on the auspicious occasion of Janaseva Trust's silver jubilee celebrations. **✅ Confirmed: 1997** — corroborated independently by ramachandrabhat.com ("Established in 1997, it offers a seven-year intensive study program in Vedas, Shastras, and Yoga along with modern subjects"). Janaseva Trust itself was founded in **1972**, so 1997 is indeed its 25th/silver jubilee year — the brochure's internal logic checks out.

### Objectives of the Gurukulam
1. To preserve the tradition of the Vedas, the Śāstras and Yoga in their original form.
2. To perpetuate the Gurukula tradition in the modern world.
3. To cultivate a social outlook, spiritual insight in the youth, and scholarship.
4. To teach the Śāstras through the right medium, i.e. saṃskṛtam.
5. To develop scholars who can converse with modern Indic scholarship and contribute to contemporary discourses.
6. To protect branches of traditional knowledge that are on the verge of extinction.

### Corner-stones of Gurukulam (Śāstra-paramparā)
The śāstra-paramparā (tradition of bhāratavarṣa) is an invaluable treasure. We believe that this paramparā can flourish only through the Gurukula tradition. Moreover, in a time when there is a growing influence of consumerism and utilitarianism driven by market forces all over the world, it is rare to find competent and committed young men and women dedicated to the cause of preserving and promoting our rich heritage. In this context, we consider it necessary to train and motivate the youth to take up this responsibility for such a mission in the modern age.

Further, we firmly believe that śāstra scholars must cultivate a scientific and social outlook and become conscious of their intellectual, cultural and social responsibilities. The Gurukulam, hence, seeks to blend and harmonize scholastic aptitudes (brāhmabala) with service orientation and dynamic capabilities (kṣātrabala) in this developing an all-round personality and not for information gathering alone.

### Blessings of Luminaries
Great luminaries like Śrī Bhāratītīrtha Mahāsannidhānam, Śaṅkarācārya of Śṛṅgeri Śāradāpīṭham have visited the Gurukulam and have showered their blessings. Other notable traditional scholars visit the Gurukulam regularly and give their inputs. International scholars in the field of Śāstra study also visit the Gurukulam. We believe that the association of students with these kinds of great people is an important element in education.

### Founder / Acharya (About → Founder — new, from ramachandrabhat.com)

**Prof. Acharya Ramachandra Bhat Kotemane (Dr. Ramachandra G Bhat)** — founder-figure behind Veda Vijnana Gurukulam.

Born in the Kotemane Agrahara of Yellapur Taluk. Completed primary education in his native village, then pursued higher studies in Gokarna, Mysuru, and other centers of learning under numerous distinguished scholars.

Has dedicated his entire life to education. For over 50 years he has made the Janaseva Parisara his *tapobhūmi*, guiding thousands of students academically and personally, and hundreds more in Sanskrit studies and research.

As a senior *Karyakarta* of the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS), he is one of the prime movers behind the **Karnataka Gurukula Prakalpa**, and has held national responsibility for the Gurukula Project under the **Bharatiya Shikshan Mandal**. His career spans school teacher to University Vice-Chancellor.

Beginning with Janaseva Trust, he has served numerous institutions dedicated to Veda Śāstras and education. He continues to teach Brahmasūtra classes at Veda Vijnana Gurukulam and Gītā Satsaṅga at S-VYASA, as Director of the **Varahamihira Vedic Research Center**.

Honors: **Bhavabhūti Puraskāra**, **Brahmarṣi Puraskāra**, among others.

### Institutional network (About — new section, "Part of a larger tradition")

VVG is not a standalone institution — it sits inside a family of institutions that reinforces credibility considerably. Worth its own About sub-section:

- **Janaseva Trust** (est. 1972) — the parent trust; runs Janaseva Vidya Kendra, a residential boys' school (650+ students, classes 5–10) blending CBSE curriculum with Gurukula values.
- **Veda Vijnana Gurukulam** (est. 1997) — this institution.
- **Veda Vijnana Shodha Samsthana** — the affiliated research institute (this is the "शोधसंस्थानम्" already in the current site's nav) — a recognized research center for Vedas, Śāstras, and Yoga, affiliated with multiple universities.
- **Maitreyi Gurukulam** (est. 1994) — sister institution for women's Vedic education, same Panchamukhi Śikṣaṇa framework (Veda, Yoga, Kṛṣi, Vijñāna, Kalā).
- **Prabodhini Gurukula** (operating since 1979, Hariharapura) — the sibling institution already studied as a design reference.
- **S-VYASA University** — Deemed University for Yoga research, founded by Padmashri Dr. H.R. Nagendra.
- Broader affiliation: **Karnataka Gurukula Prakalpa** and **Bharatiya Shikshan Mandal** — a national-level Gurukula education initiative.

### Theoretical Syllabus
1. Vedas — complete oral chanting of one Veda-śākhā and guidance for understanding the meaning.
2. Śāstras — Pūrvamīmāṃsā, Uttaramīmāṃsā (Vedānta), Vyākaraṇa, Basic Sāṅkhya-yoga, Nyāya-vaiśeṣika, Āyurveda, Sāhitya.
3. Basic English and Computers.

### Practical Syllabus
1. Yoga-āsana, Prāṇāyāma, Kriyā
2. Agriculture (Krishi)
3. Basic self-defence techniques
4. Yajña-vidhi

### Special features — education system
The education system is five-faceted: Veda, Vijñāna, Yoga, Kṛṣi (agriculture), Kalā (arts). The medium of instruction and communication is Saṃskṛtam. The competence of the students is examined by many Sabhās like Gaṇapati-sabhā, Vijaya-sabhā, Saṃkrānti-sabhā and Yugādi-sabhā, adopting Pravacana and Sālākā systems.

This maps directly onto the Design System Framework's "Subject card" component — five pillars, same pattern as Prabodhini's Panchamukhi Shikshana, confirming that's the right organism choice.
