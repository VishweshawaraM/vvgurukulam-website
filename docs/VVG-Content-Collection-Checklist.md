# VVG Content Collection Checklist

**Purpose:** Everything needed from people and places, broken into workstreams that can be split across you, your Acharya, and anyone else who joins the effort. Each workstream has a suggested owner-type, not a name — assign real people once you know who's helping.
**This is not a design or build task list** — Phase 1–7 of the Work Schedule cover that. This is purely "what has to be photographed, written down, or collected from a person."

---

## How to use this with your Acharya

Six workstreams below. Hand each one to a different person based on what they naturally have access to — the person with old photo albums shouldn't also be the one chasing alumni on WhatsApp. Each workstream lists its tasks as literal checkboxes so progress is visible at a glance when you regroup.

---

## Workstream A — Acharya / Faculty Profiles

*Best owner: whoever has a direct relationship with every current Acharya — likely you or the senior-most Acharya.*

- [ ] List every current Acharya/faculty member by name
- [ ] For each: one professional portrait photo (consistent style — same background/lighting if possible, so the Faculty page looks unified, not like a collage of random photos)
- [ ] For each: full name in English and Devanagari
- [ ] For each: designation/title (e.g. Ācārya, Principal, Guest faculty)
- [ ] For each: subjects taught
- [ ] For each: credentials/qualifications, years of service at VVG
- [ ] For each: a short bio (3–5 sentences) — background, specialization, what they teach and why it matters
- [ ] Optional: a one-line personal teaching philosophy quote per Acharya, if they're willing — this is the kind of detail that made the Founder bio compelling

**Volume:** depends on current faculty count — ask your Acharya how many people this covers before assigning it, so whoever owns it knows the scope.

---

## Workstream B — Historical & Founding Photos

*Best owner: the most senior, longest-tenured person with access to old physical albums/prints — often the hardest workstream to digitize, so start it early.*

- [ ] Founding-era photos (1997 onwards) — campus, first batch of students, founding ceremony if photographed
- [ ] Photos from each subsequent year where available, organized by year — feeds directly into the Milestone/Convocation page we already scoped
- [ ] Visits from luminaries (Śaṅkarācārya of Śṛṅgeri, other traditional scholars) — the brochure mentions these visits; photographic evidence of them is strong trust content
- [ ] Old convocation ceremony photos, year by year, as far back as they exist
- [ ] Any newspaper clippings, certificates, or press coverage — supporting evidence of credibility, not just photos

**Note:** it's fine — expected, even — if early years have thin or no photo records. Mark those years "no photo available" rather than skipping them; a partial timeline is more credible than a padded one, per the Milestone content type's design.

---

## Workstream C — Infrastructure & Campus Photos

*Best owner: someone comfortable walking the whole campus systematically with a decent camera/phone — this is the most mechanical, fastest-to-finish workstream.*

Checklist below is already known from the brochure — treat it as the actual shot list:

- [ ] Vṛṣabhācala hillock and Dakṣiṇāmūrti temple
- [ ] Library (15,000 books and palm-leaf manuscripts)
- [ ] Gośāla (the 16 indigenous cows)
- [ ] Yajñaśālā (pyramid-shaped structure)
- [ ] Guru-bhavanam (auditorium)
- [ ] Chātrāvāsas (both dormitories) — interior and exterior
- [ ] Navagraha Vana and Dhanvantari Vana (agricultural/medicinal plots)
- [ ] Dining hall / kitchen
- [ ] Classrooms in use (with students, if permission allows)
- [ ] General campus exteriors — gates, grounds, walking paths (the calm, heritage feel the design system is built around needs real photos, not stock imagery)
- [ ] Any facility not listed above that exists today but wasn't in the old brochure

**Tip:** shoot in good natural light (early morning or late afternoon), and get a few wide shots of each space plus a few close/detail shots — the design system's card and gallery components need both.

## Workstream D — Alumni Tracking

*Best owner: someone with an existing alumni network — a recent graduate still in touch with peers, or whoever runs any alumni WhatsApp/group that already exists. This is the most labor-intensive workstream since it requires outreach, not just collection.*

- [ ] Build or locate a master list of alumni by batch year (ties to the Milestone page from Workstream B)
- [ ] For each traceable alumnus: current photo
- [ ] For each: what they're doing now (organization, role, field) — the brochure already gives us the outcome *categories* (Ācāryas, Sanskrit institutions, universities, research institutes, agriculture, Yoga, teaching) — now we need actual named individuals and stories within those categories
- [ ] For each: batch year
- [ ] For each: a short quote, if they're willing to give one (this is what made Prabodhini's alumni section work)
- [ ] Get explicit consent from each alumnus before publishing their name/photo/story — standard practice, and worth doing properly since some may now hold sensitive positions

**Volume reality check:** the brochure says 100+ alumni since inception. Realistically, aim for the ~15–20 most reachable, willing, and interesting stories for launch, not all 100+ — this can grow over time through the CMS exactly like the Milestone page.

---

## Workstream E — Institutional Calendars (Daily / Monthly / Yearly plans)

*Best owner: whoever manages academic administration/scheduling — likely the Principal or a senior Acharya. This is documentation work, not photography, so it suits someone more comfortable writing than shooting photos.*

This maps to what the current live site already calls **दिनचर्या (Dinacaryā), मासचर्या (Māsacaryā), वर्षचर्या (Varṣacaryā)** — daily, monthly, and yearly routines. That structure already exists informally; this workstream is about writing it down properly:

- [ ] **Daily plan (Dinacaryā):** the full day, hour by hour, from wake-up to lights-out — this feeds the Timeline component already built for Campus Life
- [ ] **Monthly plan (Māsacaryā):** what recurs monthly — Sabhās (Gaṇapati-sabhā, Vijaya-sabhā, Saṃkrānti-sabhā, Yugādi-sabhā), monthly assessments, any monthly rituals or observances
- [ ] **Yearly plan (Varṣacaryā):** the full academic year — admission cycle (applications due end of April, entrance exams, interviews), festival calendar, seminar/workshop dates, vacation periods, convocation timing
- [ ] Any special multi-year milestones (e.g. the 7-year program structure itself — what happens in year 1 vs year 7)

---

## Workstream F — Current Student Records

*Best owner: office/administrative staff who already maintain enrollment records.*

- [ ] Current enrolled student count, confirmed against the brochure's "90 students" figure — is that still accurate?
- [ ] Student names and batch/year — **for internal admin records**

**Important — read before collecting this one:** these are minors in a residential institution. I'd strongly recommend this list stays internal to the CMS (visible to Office Staff/Principal roles only), not published as a public roster of names and photos. If you want to *show* student life publicly — and you should, it's compelling content — do it through group/activity photos and first-name-only or anonymized stories, with explicit parental consent for anything more identifying. This is worth a two-minute conversation with your Acharya and management before anyone starts collecting it, not a decision to make by default.

---

## Workstream G — Awards & Honours (new)

*Best owner: whoever has the closest relationship with the founder and senior Acharyas, since some of this involves asking them directly for certificates/details.*

- [ ] List every award/honour received by the institution (e.g. the state-level Academic Excellence award mentioned in the brochure — confirm exact years and awarding body)
- [ ] List every award/honour received by the founder (Bhavabhūti Puraskāra, Brahmarṣi Puraskāra already known — confirm year and awarding body for each)
- [ ] List any awards/honours received by individual Acharyas
- [ ] For each: a certificate scan or ceremony photo, if available
- [ ] For each: a short description of what it was awarded for

## What I can and can't do here

Being direct about the division of labor, since your Acharya asked how many people this needs:

**What only a human on the ground can do:** photographing people and places, conducting alumni outreach, gathering physical archive materials, getting consent from students/alumni/parents, and any task above requiring a camera or a phone call. None of that is something I can do for you.

**My role in this project:** I'm the content architect and coordinator on the digital side. Concretely, that means: I maintain the master schema so everything gathered above has a defined home (already done — see the Content Schema document); I organize and structure whatever raw material comes in, the way I already did with the brochure and the founder's website; I draft and polish written bios/copy from rough notes so you and your Acharyas don't have to write publication-ready English yourselves; I do supporting research and fact-verification (I already cross-checked your founding year this way); and once content arrives, I do the actual page design and build in Phase 1 onward. I'm essentially the person who turns six people's raw photos and notes into one coherent, on-brand website — but I can't be one of the six people out there taking the photos.
